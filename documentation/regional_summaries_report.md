## 1. Entire Dhaka Metropolitan Area
**Thermal Profile:** High Heat Retention & Extreme Urban Heat Island (UHI) Effect
**Average Base Temperature Estimate:** 34.5°C to 37.0°C (Summer Peaks)
**Analysis:**
Dhaka suffers from one of the most severe UHI effects in the country due to hyper-dense concrete infrastructure, vast expanses of asphalt roads, and an extreme deficiency of urban green canopy. The city acts as a massive thermal mass, absorbing solar radiation during the day and releasing it slowly at night, which prevents natural cooling. High traffic density and industrial emissions further trap heat, making the central core significantly hotter than its peripheral rural borders.

## 2. Sylhet Division
**Thermal Profile:** Cooler Micro-climates & High Canopy Ratios
**Average Base Temperature Estimate:** 30.0°C to 32.5°C
**Analysis:**
Unlike Dhaka, Sylhet features a significantly lower thermal profile. This is driven by its unique geographic composition, which includes extensive tea estates, heavy natural vegetation, and border hill tracts. The high Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) across the division ensures robust evapotranspiration—a natural process where trees release water vapor, actively cooling the surrounding air. Consequently, Sylhet's urban core experiences a much milder heat retention rate.

## 3. Rajshahi Division
**Thermal Profile:** High Summer Heat Indices & Dry Heating
**Average Base Temperature Estimate:** 36.5°C to 39.5°C (Extreme Summer Peaks)
**Analysis:**
Rajshahi geographically sits in a drier, hotter climatic zone. The region experiences extreme summer heat waves, compounded by broad expanses of bare soil and heavy pavement areas. With a lower baseline canopy density (low NDVI) compared to Sylhet, solar radiation directly bakes the surface. The lack of atmospheric moisture and reduced green cover means that heat is heavily retained in built-up areas, creating distinct, intense thermal hotspots on our GIS mapping layers.

## 4. Chittagong Division
**Thermal Profile:** Industrial Heat Belts Offset by Coastal Winds
**Average Base Temperature Estimate:** 32.5°C to 35.0°C
**Analysis:**
Chittagong presents a complex, dual-natured thermal profile. On one hand, its massive port operations, heavy industrial zones, and dense urban concrete hubs generate significant, localized heat zones (high thermal peaks). On the other hand, its geographic positioning along the coast provides consistent cooling sea breezes that naturally offset the heat buildup. This dynamic creates a highly localized UHI effect where industrial sectors glow red on the thermal map, while coastal and elevated hilly regions remain green and cool.
